Pilot v2 — from weed photos to field plans

Repositioning the pilot around the product we're actually building · АО «НЗК» · 2026

1.The shift

Pilot v1 asked: "will agronomists photograph field problems?" — which bootstrapped weed/disease recognition. That capability is real, but it is a feature, not a product: general vision models (Gemini etc.) are catching up monthly, so "better recognition" is not a defensible business. And the data premise breaks as the season cleans the fields — there are fewer problems to shoot (a cleanliness itself achieved by over-application of chemicals).

Pilot v2 repositions around the real product: turning a field's state into an efficient chemical & machine plan — and updating that plan as new observations arrive.

2.The product thesis (why this is a product, not a feature)

sense the field → produce / update a plan → execute via machines & chemicals (CropWise) → observe the outcome → revise

The moat is not recognition — it is this closed loop on a specific field over time. A general AI has none of: the field's treatment history, the CropWise execution link, the locally registered-product constraints (Госкаталог), local agronomy, or the longitudinal outcome data. Recognition is one sensor feeding the loop; the plan is the product.

3.The new data unit: a scouting pass, not a hero photo

A scouting pass tracks the weed spectrum and growth stage across the field over the season — which families are present, at what stage, how close to the economic threshold (ЭПВ). That is the data the plan needs to call the right timing, the right registered product, and the right dose.

We change the unit of collection from "a photo of a weed" to "a scouting pass that covers the field," repeated through the season. It always exists (solving "nothing to shoot") and it feeds a decision.

Corrected by the chief agronomist (and this is the pilot working): for spring crops like soy the field is sprayed whole, not by zones — weeds emerge in waves from a field-wide seed bank, most herbicides are contact (no soil residual), and there are no variable-rate sprayers. So the saving is not less area; it is the better decision — correct ЭПВ timing (one pass instead of two), the right product for the actual spectrum, and the right dose (no crop injury from over-dosing).

4.How today's work fits (nothing is thrown away)

📷 Photo shooting & recognitionPerception layer — ground-truth that calibrates field-state sensing and feeds the plan. Reframed from "hunt a problem" to "scout the field".
📝 CropWise operation loggingExecution & history layer — what was applied, where, by which machine. Already built; it is the loop's "execute + memory".
🛰️ NDVI watch / satelliteField-scale sensing (free tier) — coarse anomaly signal today; the drone layer comes next.

The pilot doesn't restart — it recomposes what exists into the plan loop.

5.New success metric

Replace "15–30 photos / week" with: a better decision that costs less chemistry — the right ЭПВ timing (fewer passes), the right registered product for the spectrum, and the right dose (no crop injury) — measured against what was actually applied, on 1–2 pilot fields, plus the chief agronomist's judgment that the plan matches how he really works. Measurable now from the CropWise spray records.

6.Roadmap

NOW

Field-plan generator on existing data + scouting-pass capture. Quantify the plan-vs-blanket saving on 1–2 fields.

NEXT

One contracted drone flight on one field as a field-scale sensing proof (manual analysis — no pipeline yet).

LATER

Close the loop: versioned per-field plans that update on each pass, with measured efficacy of what was done.

7.What we need to run it

  • Pick 1–2 pilot fields to run the full loop on.
  • Agronomist runs a scouting pass (covers the field, clean areas included) instead of chasing problems.
  • We generate a treatment plan and compare it to what was actually sprayed → the saving number.
  • Book one drone flight on one field to validate field-scale sensing.
